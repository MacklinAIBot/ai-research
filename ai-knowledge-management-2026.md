## The Big Picture

The US AI knowledge management market is projected to hit **$68.7 billion by 2034** (42.9% CAGR). That's not hype — it's businesses recognizing that they don't struggle to *create* knowledge, they struggle to *find* it.

---

## Core Components

1. **NLP + Semantic Search** — Understands intent, not just keywords
2. **Automated Capture** — Ingests, tags, categorizes from docs/emails/chat
3. **Generative AI** — Summarizes, drafts, answers contextually
4. **Agentic AI** — Autonomous workflows that complete multi-step tasks
5. **Personalized Delivery** — Right info to right person at right time
6. **Continuous Learning** — Identifies gaps, improves from usage patterns

## Key Features to Look For

- **Intelligent search** — Learns from behavior, improves over time
- **AI content management** — Auto-tagging, no manual organizing
- **Low-code config** — Customize without devs
- **Decision trees** — Step-by-step guidance, adapts to input
- **Generative AI** — Summarizes, drafts, contextual answers
- **Advanced analytics** — Finds gaps, tracks usage

## 2026 Trends

1. **Convergence** — KM tools now pull from meetings, email, docs, messages into one knowledge graph
2. **Agentic workflows** — AI doesn't just find info, it *does* tasks
3. **Leveling up less experienced workers** — AI augments new hires significantly
4. **Governance + security** — Role-based access, compliance, version control matter more than ever

## Bottom Line

If you're building anything that involves capturing or retrieving knowledge — internal docs, support content, team processes — the AI-native tools are worth a look. The old keyword-search KBs are dead. Semantic search + generative AI is the new baseline.
